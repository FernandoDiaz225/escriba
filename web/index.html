About Escriba

Escriba is a free workbench built for Apóstol Bernabé Garcia's tape ministry. It helps a small team turn a large archive of cassette recordings into clean transcripts and good-sounding listening masters, as efficiently as possible.

Everything runs on your own computer. Your audio and transcripts are never uploaded — transcription is free and private. The only thing that can be shared is anonymous usage counts, and only if you agreed when you first opened the app. Finished work is saved automatically so it's never lost, and can also sync to a shared team folder.

The work splits into four stations, shown in the tabs above: Prepare (join + clean), Transcribe (timestamped transcript), Master (listening restoration), and Library (audit + everything you've made).

How to use it

A typical tape, start to finish
  1. Audit it first (Library tab). Drop the tape in, listen for a few seconds, and flag its quality. If it's damaged or the wrong recording, you just saved yourself an hour.
  2. Prepare the audio (Prepare tab). If the sermon is split across Side A and Side B, drop both in to join them into one file. Leave "light cleanup" on. Click Prepare.
  3. Transcribe (Transcribe tab, or "Transcribe this →" from Prepare). Pick the language, click Transcribe, and watch the words appear live. Stop early if something's wrong. When it's done, download the format you need.
  4. Make a listening master (Master tab) — only if the tape is worth it. Not every tape needs this. For ones people will actually listen to, restore a clean-sounding version.
Joining Side A + Side B

In the Prepare tab, drop Side A in the first box and Side B in the second. Escriba stitches them into one continuous file. This replaces fiddling with GarageBand and gives you a clean, repeatable join every time.

Restoring a listening master

This one is hands-on, and that's on purpose — there's no magic "fix it" button that produces a good master, because every tape's noise is different. The method professionals use, which Escriba follows:

  1. Drop the tape in the Master tab.
  2. Drag across a quiet moment on the waveform — a gap between sentences, or the silent lead-in. This teaches Escriba what this tape's noise sounds like, so it removes only that.
  3. Click Preview master, then compare Before and After with the two players.
  4. Tune if needed. More noise reduction is not better — too much makes voices sound watery. Start low.
  5. When it sounds right, name it and Save master. You get both a high-quality WAV and a shareable MP3.
First time? Pick one tape and settle on settings you like before doing the whole archive — that's what audio archivists recommend, and it saves redoing work.
Where your work goes

Every transcript and master is saved automatically to a library on your computer (and listed in the Library tab). Set a Shared folder there to also copy everything into your team's Google Drive or Dropbox, so the whole team stays in sync.

Standard vs Fast, and languages

Use Standard mode unless your computer has an NVIDIA graphics card. For language, pick Spanish or English, or let it auto-detect. If a tape has a live interpreter, choose the language you want to keep — and if you can, record that language on its own track for the cleanest result.

A quick note on usage stats

Escriba is free and runs on your computer. To understand how it's used and keep improving it, it can share anonymous usage stats. Your choice — it works fully either way.

  • Shares: an anonymous ID, how many transcriptions you run, and total minutes
  • Never shares: your audio, transcripts, file names, or anything personal
